Visiting Brittany by campervan, van or car means admiring breathtaking views, and for us it's become a destination we consider **{{< bold "unmissable">}}**. Want some tips on how to visit this French region on the road? In this article we'll tell you about our experience in September 2022.
If you're thinking of visiting it together with Normandy, you can find our stops [here](/blog/normandy-by-campervan-itinerary/).

## Itinerary
In this section we'll list all the stops of our adventure.
If you want to read our itinerary in short, you'll find it [here](#in-short), while if you want to know our thoughts and which stops we consider **unmissable**, you can [click here](#reflections-and-unmissable-stops).

**{{< underline "Day 1">}}**: first stop **{{< bold "Mont Saint-Michel">}}**. We arrived early in the morning at a small kiosk's car park, where you can park for a couple of hours if you buy breakfast there. Having bought coffee and chocolate madeleines, we enjoyed both the treats and the view. Then, having secured the animals in the van to spare them the crowds we'd be facing, we set off on about an hour's walk. If you don't feel like walking, you can queue for the paid little train. Having already visited the Abbey years before, we just walked through the little streets, in our opinion a bit spoiled by too many touristy signs, before heading to a car park in **{{< bold "Cherrueix">}}**, right on the seafront.

**{{< underline "Day 2">}}**: in the afternoon we moved to **{{< bold "Dinan">}}**, a small medieval village with a fairy-tale atmosphere, walking through the little streets all the way to the small harbour. For the night we moved to the free stopover area in **{{< bold "Léhon">}}**.

{{< leggi-anche >}}

**{{< underline "Day 3">}}**: in Léhon we visited the abbey, surrounded by a roofless portico, with a photography exhibition in the cloister. Then, caught by a storm, we headed to **{{< bold "Saint-Malo">}}**. Strolling aimlessly, we stepped into a church rich in Gothic details, with a wonderful rose window above the apse, before continuing along the ramparts. After heading back to the van, we moved to a small car park overlooking the bay for lunch, then headed towards **{{< bold "Cap Fréhel">}}**. The wind was extremely strong. We managed to glimpse the lighthouse through fog and rain before shutting ourselves inside. We stayed overnight for free.

**{{< underline "Day 4">}}**: we wanted to visit nearby **{{< bold "Fort la Latte">}}**, but it was closed due to bad weather, so we settled for a walk on the cliffs during a brief break from the rain. Then we got back on the road, stopping for a walk on the beach given the low tide, before reaching a car park overlooking the bay of **{{< bold "Langueux">}}**. We stayed overnight for free.

**{{< underline "Day 5">}}**: we reached **{{< bold "Beauport Abbey">}}**, which has a truly striking presence. The roof is almost entirely missing, and all around, nature reigns supreme, with trees, rocks, a river running through and colourful hydrangeas. We then moved to the nearby small village of **{{< bold "Paimpol">}}**, wandering the streets aimlessly, reaching the small harbour and stepping into an antiques shop, full of sets of cotton bedsheets, cups, plates and glasses. A few kilometres later it was the turn of **{{< bold "Tréguier">}}**, a medieval-style village. We stayed overnight for free.

**{{< underline "Day 6">}}**: we woke up early to visit the house nestled among the rocks, **{{< bold "Castel Meur">}}**, in **{{< bold "Plougrescant">}}**. Then, due to rain, we moved to the **{{< bold "Ploumanac'h">}}** lighthouse, beautiful despite the bad weather. In the late afternoon we moved to **{{< bold "Morlaix">}}**, famous for its majestic viaduct, and then to **{{< bold "Roscoff">}}**. We stayed overnight for free.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto,w_800,dpr_auto/v1682806087/Articoli/francia/IMG_5489_w4edks.jpg">}}

**{{< underline "Day 7">}}**: a trip to **{{< bold "Île de Batz">}}**, a small gem reachable by ferry from the town of Roscoff. Here time really seems to have stopped, between white beaches, cobbled paths, unkempt fields, wild horses. It genuinely stayed close to our hearts. If you decide to visit, we recommend buying a filled baguette at one of Roscoff's bakeries for lunch and enjoying a picnic in nature. Back at the van, we reached the **{{< bold "Kermorvan">}}** lighthouse and then headed towards **{{< bold "Pointe Saint-Mathieu">}}**.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto,w_800,dpr_auto/v1682806078/Articoli/francia/IMG_5772_oizi8q.jpg">}}

**{{< underline "Day 8">}}**: we visited the **{{< bold "Saint-Mathieu">}}** lighthouse and the nearby roofless abbey, then headed to **{{< bold "Quimper">}}** and for the night to **{{< bold "Fouesnant">}}**, at a car park overlooking the bay.

**{{< underline "Day 9">}}**: we stayed in the small village chatting with friendly passers-by, then headed to **{{< bold "Concarneau">}}** (don't miss one of the famous butter tarts) and finally moved on to **{{< bold "Pont-Aven">}}**, sleeping right in nature near the **{{< bold "Trémalo">}}** chapel.

**{{< underline "Day 10">}}**: we visited the village of Pont-Aven, with its well-kept centre, taking advantage of a small local farmers' market. Then we moved to **{{< bold "Quimperlé">}}**, and finally in the evening to the outskirts of Vannes.

**{{< underline "Day 11">}}**: we visited **{{< bold "Vannes">}}** in the rain, and without letting it discourage us, we strolled through the little streets all the way to the Jardin de la Préfecture, then found a great car park in the woods at Chavagne, heading towards Rennes.

**{{< underline "Day 11">}}**: after visiting **{{< bold "Rennes">}}** we headed to **{{< bold "Vitré">}}**, a lovely little village.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto,w_800,dpr_auto/v1682804772/Articoli/francia/IMG_3897_ytghyv.jpg">}}

From here on our adventures continued south, still following the Atlantic coast. We crossed the region called Pays de la Loire, making only a couple of stops: one at Moisdon-la-Rivière (a beautiful, tiny village surrounded by lush nature, south of Châteaubriant) and the other at Nantes (unfortunately it was pouring with rain, but we still managed to see the elephant statue, inspired by Jules Verne and Leonardo, which trumpets and sprays water as it moves), leaving the visit to the Loire castles for a future trip.
Finally we headed into [Nouvelle-Aquitaine](/blog/nouvelle-aquitaine-campervan-itinerary/), all the way to the regional capital Bordeaux and from there to Northern Spain (article on that coming soon).

## Roads
As in the rest of the country, we didn't find any roads in dangerous condition. All the areas near the lighthouses offer truly breathtaking views.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto,w_800,dpr_auto/v1682806085/Articoli/francia/IMG_4801_mof0mc.jpg">}}

## Services and stops
In France it's very easy to find equipped, free service areas for water fill-up and waste disposal.
We had no trouble with free overnight stays since we travelled in the low season. The only thing we'd recommend paying attention to is places where the wind could be a nuisance or even dangerous.
If you go in summer you might find it harder to find parking.

## Reflections and unmissable stops
This was our first experience in Brittany, and we truly loved this region. Even though the weather isn't the most favourable, you can feel a magical atmosphere from another era.

By campervan or car, in our opinion the unmissable destinations are:
-   **{{< bold "Mont Saint-Michel">}}**: even though by now it's, in our opinion, too touristy a destination, it's definitely worth a visit.
-   **{{< bold "Dinan - Léhon">}}**: both Dinan and the small hamlet of Léhon are beautiful.
-   **{{< bold "the lighthouses">}}**: Cap Fréhel, Fort la Latte, the Ploumanac'h lighthouse, the Kermorvan lighthouse, the Saint-Mathieu lighthouse... genuinely all wonderful, even wrapped in fog.
-   **{{< bold "Castel Meur, in Plougrescant">}}**: the small house is no longer reachable up close due to an accident years ago, but the surrounding setting is worth the detour!
-   **{{< bold "Île de Batz">}}**: this small island won us over from the very first step. Not to be missed.
-   **{{< bold "Vannes and Rennes">}}**: two very beautiful, tidy cities, without losing sight of the surrounding countryside and small nearby villages, like Vitré.
