Ariège is a department of the Occitanie region, located in the heart of the Pyrenees in southern France. We discovered it by chance coming from [Andorra](/blog/andorra-campervan-weekend-breathtaking-views/) and heading towards [Carcassonne](/blog/carcassonne-medieval-town-board-game/), and we **immediately fell in love** with its landscapes of unspoiled nature and villages straight out of the Middle Ages, all framed by majestic mountains.

{{< indice >}}

## Between nature and tradition
Ariège is a place that satisfies everyone: those who love nature and outdoor sports, those who love prehistoric sites and natural landmarks (Ariège has several caves you can visit to admire cave paintings), history buffs (many Cathars took refuge in the castles of this area to escape the Catholic inquisition), and those who prefer small villages to the chaos of big cities.

It's also a region **rich in culinary tradition**, populated by people smiling in the streets of every village while waving baguettes, and perfect for those travelling on the road.

## Unmissable stops
Below are the stops that excited us most in Ariège; to follow our 9-day itinerary, head to the dedicated section (which you'll find by clicking [here](#itinerary)). Buckle up, because in Ariège it's all quite a **rollercoaster of ups and downs**, and get your camera ready to capture some truly exciting views!

### Ax-les-Thermes
A pleasant mountain town, known for its thermal waters and ski slopes. 2 km away is the small village of ***Savignac-les-Ormeaux***, where you can enjoy a beautiful nature walk following the lake's perimeter and the riverbank.

### Tarascon-sur-Ariège
A truly characteristic medieval village, well worth a stroll through streets leading to a small viewpoint and the old Clock Tower.

### Foix
The very characteristic town is famous for the {{< extLink "castle" "https://www.sites-touristiques-ariege.fr/chateau-de-foix/">}}, whose origins date back to the 10th century, and whose perched position allowed the city to withstand the crusade against the Cathars.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/v1690117442/Articoli/ariege/ariege_foix_mnxl7b.jpg">}}

### La Bastide-de-Sérou
A small, picturesque village, offering lovely walks in the surrounding area. Don't miss a stop in nearby ***Durban-sur-Arize***, where we bought wood-fired bread from a young baker who loves his craft. If you can't find the shop, just follow your nose (or {{< extLink "click here" "https://goo.gl/maps/MqDj7QcR6qZJn66CA">}})!

### Saint-Lizier
This beautiful town has the prestige of hosting the seat of an important bishopric. What struck us was its lovely historic centre, cared for down to every detail and full of artisan shops. If you have time, visit nearby ***Montjoie-en-Couserans*** for a walk through a village stuck in another era.

### Le Mas-d'Azil
This place is known for the **{{< extLink "Grotte du Mas-d'Azil" "https://www.sites-touristiques-ariege.fr/roadtrip-de-site-en-site/grotte-du-mas-dazil/">}}**. It's a mystical place: besides being able to cross the cave not only on foot but also on 2 or 4 wheels, it lets you discover the prehistoric life of the Cro-Magnons. Don't miss a walk among the colourful half-timbered houses of the village of the same name a little further on, from where numerous nature walks depart, including some to ancient dolmens.

### Camon and Carla-Bayle
A lovely fortified village home to numerous **artists' workshops**, overlooking the lake that shares its name, along whose shores you can enjoy a lovely nature walk. If you find it open, don't miss the church's paintings — truly unusual.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/v1690117444/Articoli/ariege/ariege_carla_bayle_jqqoul.jpg">}}

### Montségur
A beautiful mountain village reached via a paved climb nestled between the mountains, at the end of which the first wood-and-stone houses appear, along with a single grocery shop, a small restaurant, a tiny church and a wonderful panorama.

Here you'll also find the imposing {{< extLink "Cathar castle" "http://www.montsegur.fr/">}}. If you want to visit the ruins of Montségur castle, keep in mind it's reached by at least half an hour's uphill walk.

### Lac de Montbel
A lake with a 16-km perimeter, offering wonderful walks along the shores as well as the chance for a refreshing swim.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/v1690117448/Articoli/ariege/lac_montbel_iajj4k.jpg">}}

### Mirepoix
Considered one of the most beautiful villages in France, **Mirepoix** is a small medieval town with half-timbered houses and wooden galleries hiding restaurants and shops. The Cathedral is wonderful, with rose windows overlooking a large nave. On Monday mornings the village hosts the region's most famous market. Our verdict? Delightful, but spoiled by too much tourism. That's why we recommend a stop, even if brief, in nearby ***Camon***, a fortified village famous as the village of roses and visible from afar thanks to its imposing castle and abbey — decidedly more authentic.

### Carcassonne
A typical medieval town, ranked among the 5 most visited cities in France, with a double set of walls, 4 gates and 52 towers visible from a distance, conjuring up images of knights and ladies. We talked more about our visit and why we went there [in this article](/blog/carcassonne-medieval-town-board-game/). If you have time left, head to nearby **Fanjeaux**, a small medieval village where we visited a beautiful exhibition of painting and sculpture.

## Itinerary
Below is the 9-day itinerary that most excited us in Ariège:

**{{< underline "Day 1">}}**: Ax-les-Thermes and Tarascon-sur-Ariège

**{{< underline "Day 2">}}**: Foix and its castle

**{{< underline "Day 3">}}**: La Bastide-de-Sérou and Saint-Lizier

**{{< underline "Day 4">}}**: Le Mas-d'Azil and the cave

**{{< underline "Day 5">}}**: Carla-Bayle and the lake

**{{< underline "Day 6">}}**: Montségur and the Cathar castle

**{{< underline "Day 7">}}**: Lac de Montbel

**{{< underline "Day 8">}}**: Mirepoix and Camon

**{{< underline "Day 9">}}**: Carcassonne

In our itinerary, improvised on the spot (but which gave us so many great moments), we skipped **Toulouse** because reviews about campervan parking weren't the most reassuring. There would have been many more stops worth considering, between small villages and natural sites. Unfortunately we couldn't stay longer in this French region, which nonetheless let us admire all its splendour in just a few days.
If you cross Ariège to reach Andorra or Spain, or on your way back, we recommend a stop in **L'Hospitalet**, a small village nestled among woods and mountains.

We took advantage of the calm and serenity that Ariège gives off everywhere to fall in love once again with the French countryside, and once again not only thanks to the sweetness of [pain au chocolat!](/blog/pain-au-chocolat-o-chocolatine-la-miglior-colazione).
