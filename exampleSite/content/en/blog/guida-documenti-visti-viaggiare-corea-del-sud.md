Travelling to South Korea **is an exciting experience**, but it requires proper preparation when it comes to documents and permits. Requirements vary depending on nationality, length of stay and purpose of travel. Here's a complete overview to help you plan your trip with peace of mind.

## Passport: The first requirement
To enter South Korea you always need a **valid passport**. Make sure it:
- Has at least **3 months** of remaining validity from the date of arrival for tourist stays.
- Has at least **6 months** of remaining validity for long stays (e.g. study or work).

Check your passport well in advance to avoid surprises!

## K-ETA: Electronic authorisation for short stays
Citizens of many countries can enter South Korea **without a visa** for stays of up to **90 days** (tourism, business or transit). However, in many cases the **{{< extLink "Korea Electronic Travel Authorization (K-ETA)" "https://www.k-eta.go.kr/portal/newapply/index.do" >}}** is required, an electronic authorisation to be obtained online before departure.

From 1 April 2023 to 31 December 2025, **the K-ETA requirement is suspended for stays under 90 days**. Always check for official updates before you leave.

### How K-ETA works
- **Online application**: Complete the application on the official K-ETA website.
- **Cost**: About 10,000 KRW (7-8 USD).
- **Validity**: Once approved, it's linked to your passport and valid for multiple entries within 2 years.

## Visa for Long Stays
If you plan to stay in South Korea for **more than 90 days**, you'll need to apply for a **visa** at the South Korean embassy or consulate in your country. Visa types include:
- Study visa.
- Work visa.
- Business visa.

Contact the embassy in advance to find out which documents you need and how long processing takes.

## Driving in South Korea
If you plan to drive during your stay, you'll need:
- An **international driving permit** (Vienna or Geneva Convention), to be requested in Italy at the Motorizzazione Civile, or the equivalent authority in your country. We've written [an article about the process](https://vandipety.it/blog/patente-internazionale-guida-completa-requisiti-costi) (in Italian).
- **Your national licence**: The international permit isn't valid on its own, so always carry both.

To rent a car or campervan, a **credit card** may be required as a guarantee.

## Health Insurance: A must for travellers
South Korea has a high-quality healthcare system, but foreign tourists aren't covered by Korea's National Health Insurance. It's therefore **essential** to take out **private health insurance** covering:
- **Medical expenses** for illness or accidents (a minimum of 50,000 USD is recommended).
- **Medical emergencies** with 24/7 assistance.
- **Unexpected events** such as trip cancellation or lost luggage.

## Conclusion
Getting the right documents together is the first step to enjoying a worry-free trip to South Korea. Check your passport's validity, find out whether you need a K-ETA or a visa, bring your international driving permit if you plan to drive, and take out adequate health insurance.
