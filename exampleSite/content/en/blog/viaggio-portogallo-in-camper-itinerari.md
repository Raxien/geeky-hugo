We visited Portugal by campervan with our dog and cat in December 2022, in a **period not much travelled by tourism**, and therefore with more ease in finding a spot to stay (keep in mind free camping has been banned in this country since 2021!). The weather wasn't the most favourable, but we still got to admire beautiful places!

We decided to split the itineraries into 4 regions: the north and Porto, the centre and Lisbon, the Alentejo coast, and the south with the Algarve. In this article we share the stops and useful tips for visiting the country, including with your pet, pointing you to the specific articles for details on each town or place to visit.

## Roads
In Portugal you'll find motorways (AE), expressways, split into 'Itinerários Principais' (IP) and 'Itinerários Complementares' (IC), national roads (EN) and local roads.

To drive on motorways you pay a toll, which can be collected at a tollbooth, where present, or only electronically.

On motorways with tollbooths, payment is made in cash or by credit or debit card; there's also another payment method, Via Verde, via bank direct debit, for those with an electronic device bought at the dedicated {{<extLink "sales points" "https://www.viaverde.pt">}}.
The Via Verde system is also available for vehicles with foreign plates thanks to Via Verde Visitors, a rental device that lets you drive and pay at all toll road infrastructure (including those with an exclusively electronic system, and bridges).

We only drove on local and national roads, although on some stretches they weren't in the best condition.

## Services and stops
Unlike other European countries, it's not that easy to find equipped stopover areas (especially in the south), and it's especially hard to find water fill-up points. You will, however, find plenty of public fountains to fill bottles and water bottles.
Alternatively, you can ask campsites to just use the paid facilities.

Since 2021, free camping has been strictly forbidden. During autumn and winter it's "tolerated" in some areas. Camping is always banned: the police will fine you even for just a chair left outside!

## Reflections and unmissable stops
By campervan or car, in our opinion the unmissable destinations are:
-   **{{< bold "Viana do Castelo">}}**: a genuinely lovely town. Bad weather takes away some of its charm, but if you can, stroll through its streets without rushing.
-   **{{< bold "Porto">}}**: we saw Porto with a huge amount of construction work going on. It genuinely embodies the Portuguese spirit of saudade.
-   **{{< bold "Coimbra">}}**: a lovely town; we loved strolling through both its parks and its little streets. Seeing the students in uniform makes you want to go straight back to school!
-   **{{< bold "Cabo da Roca">}}**: moving at any time of day, but if you happen to walk along the cliffs when the curious crowds trying to capture just the sunset have left, or early in the morning... it offers stunning views and makes you understand the urge to go looking for what's beyond the sea!
-   **{{< bold "Lisbon">}}**: a lively, elegant city offering plenty to do and see.
-   **{{< bold "Alentejo">}}**: less touristy, but if you love nature and the coast, it's the place for you.
-   **{{< bold "Algarve">}}**: we didn't love the towns in this region, but the cliffs are definitely worth a visit.
