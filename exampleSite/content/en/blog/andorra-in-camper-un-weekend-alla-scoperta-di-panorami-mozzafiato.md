We'd decided to cross the Principality of Andorra by campervan for two reasons: to find some relief from the heat in the mountains, and to add a flag (**number 17!**) to the countries visited together with Sakè, our globetrotting kitten, and of course Olimpia, our rottweiler with a sweet face but a strong-willed character.

Our entry into Andorra wasn't the smoothest, thanks to a wasp that gave us a nasty quarter of an hour and sent us on a hospital detour before the border. Looking back, that episode is actually pretty funny — if you want to see it, we captured it all {{<extLink "in this video" "https://youtu.be/VJD3v8a7K7M">}}.

## Andorra: what to visit by campervan in a weekend
**Andorra la Vella** is the capital of Andorra, sitting at 1,023 m altitude and nicknamed the **"Capital of the Pyrenees"**. Although everywhere you're told not to miss the Plaça del Poble and admire Saint Stephen's Church from the outside, we much preferred reaching the nearby ***Plaça de la Rotonda***, where you can admire the ***Noblesa del Temps***, a surrealist bronze sculpture by Salvador Dalí overlooking the river, made up of angels and demons and a typical melting clock.

After the obligatory photo, we left all the city concrete behind to take refuge in the greenery of the surrounding mountains.

{{< image src="https://res.cloudinary.com/ilgattodicitturin/image/upload/v1690038413/Articoli/andorra/andorra_orologio_dal%C3%AC_i6mapq.jpg">}}

If you'd rather stay in Andorra la Vella town, you can visit the ***Casa de la Vall***, home to the National Parliament, stroll along the ***Passeig del Rec del Solà trail***, admire boutiques and jewellery shops on Meritxell Avenue, take a tour around the various shopping centres, head to the ***Caldea thermal complex*** (the largest, and according to many the most beautiful, in Europe), stop just half an hour from the city to reach nearby Meritxell and visit the ***Sanctuary of Our Lady***, go all in on adrenaline at ***Naturlandia***, an amusement park where you can spot wolves, bears, lynxes, deer, marmots and ibexes, or ride the world's longest toboggan run, jump on the Xtreme Jumps and much more.

## Where to stay overnight by campervan
Here are the four spots where we stayed overnight:

- The first spot we parked at, {{<extLink "in Sant Julià de Lòria" "https://goo.gl/maps/J5u94eF4v3XRi8Pj9">}}, was the one by the shopping centre. It's very close to the Spanish border and allows free water fill-up and waste disposal. A concrete lot, but better than nothing!

- In Andorra la Vella town we parked {{<extLink "here" "https://goo.gl/maps/pPkqenSwxGZxymo39">}} for free between 1pm and 3pm, reaching the city centre in a 10-minute walk.

- This was {{<extLink "our favourite spot" "https://goo.gl/maps/nv2T8HCR6X2eK3KPA">}} for two days right in nature. You can go for walks and cool off in the river. The maximum stay allowed is 48 hours.

- This {{<extLink "free car park" "https://goo.gl/maps/qAvFx5HbAHB6vByTA">}} for campervans is very handy as a last stop before crossing into France, or right after leaving the country having visited Ariège<!-- (se non conosci questa regione devi subito rimediare) -->. It's located near a couple of ski resorts, where you'll also find free wifi.

## Important note
Andorra is famous for its status as a tax haven, which encourages **duty-free trade**. Remember to fill up on petrol or diesel in this Principality: in July 2023 we paid 1.2 euros per litre!
If you enjoy cigarettes you'll also find them here by the carton at great prices, along with liquor and chocolate (we paid 5 euros for 1 kg of dark chocolate — a real treat).
